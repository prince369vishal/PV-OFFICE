The gap no one talks about

Why Students Fail in Their First Job

They know how to code. They don't know how companies actually work.

01

No idea how Jira works

Can't navigate tickets, sprint boards, or task workflows — the daily tools of every dev team.

02

No code review experience

Panic when a senior leaves comments on a PR. Never learned to give or receive technical feedback.

03

Poor team communication

Struggle to write clear status updates, escalate blockers, or talk to a manager about progress.

04

Can't navigate a codebase

Totally lost when dropped into a large existing project. Only ever built things from scratch.

05

No deadline experience

Never shipped under real pressure. Time estimates, sprint commitments, and urgency are all new.

06

Zero production exposure

Only tutorials and solo projects. No idea what deploying, monitoring, or incidents look like.

These aren't personality flaws — they're exposure gaps. Every single one is fixable in 30 days of real simulation.
